Why digital signage?

Save time and money vs. printed signage

Dynamic vs. static content

Modern look (graphics and animations)

Centralized control to manage multiple locations

Imagine we have a franchise store that has printed signage material (our product lineup, pricing list, sign advertisements around the store, etc). Let's say a month down the road our pricing structure changes; a month after that we now offer discounts when a customer purchases a combination of products; two months after that we decide to scratch the discounts; over the holidays we want to spotlight specific products relevant to that holiday, etc. Each time we go through this, we are faced with the task of updating printed materials. Moving to dynamic digital signage would save time and money over static printed materials. Initial investment may be more, but our long term future is brighter. Next imagine we have to maintain 20, 50, even 100 different locations. Having centralized control over digital signage would make updating even easier.

Why we tried Drupal...

High cost and increasing frustration with current non-Drupal options

In the past year, we have been approached by numerous end users and content manages on campus who have expressed frustration and concern with their Visix and/or FourWinds displays. It was difficult for them to manage their content (either adding new content, updating existing content, or removing content). Some users even turned off their displays. The price was too much for what some users felt they were receiving / producing. Keep in mind, I am not going to include costs for the display itself, mounting hardware and location setup from facilities management, since these will be the same across the board for all options. The costs involved above are above and beyond these costs.

CAS allows us to utilize the university's authentication system for logins. Feeds allows us to listen to the RSS feeds for news and events from the College and departments to auto-populate the news and events on the digital signs. Content managers can enter a news item or event in one location and numerous additional locations can display the item. Organic Groups are the backbone of the digital signage. This allows us to create a group which has its own 'homepage'. The group 'homepage' becomes the digital sign for the group. For instance, we create a group called 'Chemistry'. The group homepage becomes http://clas.uiowa.edu/signs/chemistry. We then assign CAS users to the group as content managers where needed. Panelizer - If OG is the backbone, then Panelizer is the brain. Panelizer allows us to let content managers decide which widgets they want to use and where (within limits) the widgets will appear. I'll show you more during the live demo shortly. Panels and Views - These are pretty self-explanatory. Panels for drag and drop content management and Views for viewing content how we want.

BONGO uses the BONGO API to get information about bus routes and schedules for the University's Cambus system as well as the Iowa City and Coralville transportation systems. Scrolling News uses Javascript to display news items similar to a CNN or ESPN ticker at the bottom of the display. Rotating Events uses Javascript to alternate between College and Department events. Department Directory Listing pulls department information from the University White Pages and displays specific information we determine. The issue with this is that the University White Pages prevent us from differentiating between active and inactive graduate students as well as staff vs. faculty in some case. This will hope to be remedied with retrieval of specific data from a new University system.

Weather Forecast

Create a shell script (forecast.sh) to pull the forecast from Weather Underground® and create a local forecast.json file:


#!/bin/sh
mv -f <path>/forecast.json <path>/forecast.json.bak
curl -s http://api.wunderground.com/api/<WeatherUndergroundID>/forecast/geolookup/
	conditions/q/IA/Iowa_City.json?pws=0 -o "<path>/forecast.json"

Weather Forecast

Create a cronjob to run the forecast.sh script on a schedule:


MAILTO="email@domain.edu"
*/3 * * * * root <path>/forecast.sh >/dev/null 2>&1

Hawk Alert (Emergency Notification System)

Hawk Alert listens to the University’s emergency notification system for any threats to physical safety in emergency situations (tornado, violence, hazardous material incidents, etc). In addition to phone calls, text messages and website notifications, we wanted to use these displays for additional notifications.

Hawk Alert

Create a shell script (hawkalert.sh) to pull the Hawk Alert CAP Feed and create a local hawkalert.xml file:


#!/bin/sh
mv -f <path>/hawkalert.xml <path>/hawkalert.xml.bak
curl -s http://emergency.uiowa.edu/hawk-alert-CAP -o "<path>/hawkalert.xml"

Hawk Alert
