Why digital signage?

Save time and money vs. printed signage

Dynamic vs. static content

Timely (even immediate) updates to content

Centralized control to manage multiple locations

Modern look (graphics and animations)

Imagine we have an event in the Biology Department that we want to draw attention to. Rather than printing out dozens of flyers and sending out mass emails drawing attention to the event, we can produce a digital flyer and display that on numerous displays around campus. We've save time, money, trees, etc over the previous alternative.

Printed Signage

Mass produced, shipped from a central location, requires an employee to update the local sign/menu/board.

Digital Menu Signage

Created by central marketing, pushed out to remote locations, updated automatically.

Why we tried Drupal...

High cost and increasing frustration with current non-Drupal options

In the several years, we have been approached by numerous end users and content managers on campus who have expressed frustration and concern with their Visix and/or FourWinds displays. It was difficult for them to manage their content (either adding new content, updating existing content, or removing content). Some users even turned off their displays. The price was too much for what some users felt they were receiving / producing.

CAS allows us to utilize the university's authentication system for logins. Feeds allows us to listen to the RSS feeds for news and events from the College and departments to auto-populate the news and events on the digital signs. Content managers can enter a news item or event in one location and numerous additional locations can display the item. Organic Groups are the backbone of the digital signage. This allows us to create a group which has its own 'homepage'. The group 'homepage' becomes the digital sign for the group. For instance, we create a group called 'Chemistry'. The group homepage becomes http://clas.uiowa.edu/signs/chemistry. We then assign CAS users to the group as content managers where needed. Panels and Views - These are pretty self-explanatory. Panels for drag and drop content management with the In-Place Editor (IPE) and Views for viewing content how we want.

Weather Forecast

Create a shell script (forecast.sh) to pull the forecast from Weather Underground® and create a local forecast.json file:


#!/bin/sh
mv -f <path>/forecast.json <path>/forecast.json.bak
curl -s http://api.wunderground.com/api/<WeatherUndergroundID>/forecast/geolookup/
  conditions/q/IA/Iowa_City.json?pws=0 -o "<path>/forecast.json"

Weather Forecast

Create a cronjob to run the forecast.sh script on a schedule:


MAILTO="email@domain.edu"
*/3 * * * * root <path>/forecast.sh >/dev/null 2>&1

Weather Forecast

Tell the JavaScript to read from the local forecast.json file:


function displayWeather() {

      type : 'GET',

      }
